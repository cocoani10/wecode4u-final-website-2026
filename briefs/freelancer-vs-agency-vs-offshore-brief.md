## 3. Search Intent

**Decisional / Commercial Investigation**

The searcher is a founder, CTO, or ops lead who has a project in mind and is evaluating whether to hire a freelancer, engage an agency, or set up an offshore team. They are not looking for definitions — they already understand these models. They want a fast, honest framework to make the call. They are 1–3 weeks from a buying decision.

**Do NOT write for someone who doesn't know what these models are.**
Write for someone who knows, but hasn't decided.

---

**Secondary:** Operations leaders at mid-market companies considering their first outsourcing decision
**Tone calibration:** Peer-to-peer authority. The reader is smart. Write to that.

## 9. Recommended Article Structure

The article should feel like a premium Stripe or Vercel editorial: clean, opinionated, data-light but insight-heavy. Think: a well-formatted internal memo a CTO would send to their board. Not a blog post. A reference document.

**Structure principle:** Answer-first. State the conclusion of each section immediately. Then back it up.

**Section density:** Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max). No wall-of-text. Lots of white space breathing room. One strong visual per major section.

**Opening:** No preamble. Drop straight into the decision frame.

## 14. Notes for the Writing Step

### Voice
Write like a senior engineering leader talking to a peer. Compact sentences. No hedging. No "it depends" without immediately explaining what it depends on.

### What to avoid
- Do NOT start with "In today's fast-paced world..."
- Do NOT define what a freelancer is as if the reader has never heard the word
- Do NOT use passive voice in claim statements
- Do NOT use "leverage", "seamlessly", "streamline", "holistic", "robust"
- No em dashes used decoratively (—)
- No AI-tell phrases: "delve into", "it's worth noting", "at the end of the day", "game-changer"

### What to include
- Real numbers where possible (e.g., avg freelancer hourly rate $75–150, offshore team $25–60, agency project markups 40–60%)
- One sharp, opinionated claim per section that a reader could quote
- The article should make WeCode4U's model look like the obvious choice for scale — not through self-promotion, but through honest framework design

### Editorial reference
Think: **Stripe's blog** meets **a16z's founder memos**. The article doesn't feel like content marketing. It feels like the smartest person in the room wrote a memo they didn't have to publish — but did anyway.

### Structural reminder
- H1 appears once at the top
- No heading before the first paragraph — open cold
- Use `<table>` or equivalent for all comparison visuals
- Pull quote in the middle breaks the rhythm (premium editorial feel)
- End with a short, confident 2-sentence conclusion, then CTA
